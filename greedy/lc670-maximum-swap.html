📝 Problem Statement

Given a non-negative integer num, you can swap two digits at most once. Return the maximum value you can get.

🧠 Solving Approach — Greedy O(n)

For each digit (left to right), check if there's a strictly larger digit somewhere to its right. If yes, swap with the rightmost occurrence of the largest such digit and we're done. We use a last[10] array to remember the rightmost index of each digit 0–9.

🎯 Core Insight

Why leftmost mismatch? Swapping a leftmost smaller digit with a bigger one gives the biggest place-value gain.
Why rightmost occurrence? If digit 9 appears twice to the right, swap with the later one — pushes the smaller digit further right, increasing the result more.
Why try d from 9 downward? We want the LARGEST possible digit at the leftmost slot.
