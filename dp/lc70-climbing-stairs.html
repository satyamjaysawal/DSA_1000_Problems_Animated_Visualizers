📝 Problem Statement

You are climbing a staircase with n steps. Each time you can take either 1 step or 2 steps. How many distinct ways can you reach the top?

🧠 Solving Approach

Classic Fibonacci-style DP. To reach step i, you must come from step i-1 (1-step jump) or step i-2 (2-step jump). So dp[i] = dp[i-1] + dp[i-2].

🎯 Core Concepts & Base Case

Base Case: dp[1] = 1 (one way: 1-step), dp[2] = 2 (two ways: 1+1 or 2).
Recurrence: dp[i] = dp[i-1] + dp[i-2] for i ≥ 3.
Insight: Answer is the (n+1)th Fibonacci number — climbing(1)=1, climbing(2)=2, climbing(3)=3, climbing(4)=5, climbing(5)=8...
