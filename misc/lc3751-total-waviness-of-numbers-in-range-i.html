📝 Problem Statement

Given two integers num1 and num2, calculate the total waviness of all numbers in the range [num1, num2] (inclusive). The "waviness" of a number = count of peaks + valleys in its digits.

📈 Peak: A digit strictly greater than BOTH neighbors. 📉 Valley: A digit strictly less than BOTH neighbors. First and last digits NEVER count (only one neighbor each). Numbers with fewer than 3 digits → waviness = 0.

🧠 Solving Approach

Brute force simulation: iterate every number in [num1, num2], convert to string, check each middle digit against both neighbors. Add per-number waviness to running total.

🎯 Core Concepts

Peak: d[i] > d[i-1] AND d[i] > d[i+1] Valley: d[i] < d[i-1] AND d[i] < d[i+1] Skip if len(num) < 3
⏱ Time: O(N · L) 💾 Space: O(L)
