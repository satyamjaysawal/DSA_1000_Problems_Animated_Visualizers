GT LEARN #3658 🔢 GCD of Odd and Even Sums EASY Math Number Theory GCD
n= exp: 4

📝 Problem Statement

Given a positive integer n, calculate two values:

• sumOdd = sum of the smallest n positive odd numbers (1, 3, 5, ...)

• sumEven = sum of the smallest n positive even numbers (2, 4, 6, ...)

Return GCD(sumOdd, sumEven) — the greatest common divisor.

🧠 Solving Approach

Simulation — accumulate both sums in two loops, then apply Euclidean algorithm to find GCD: repeatedly replace (a, b) with (b, a % b) until b = 0. Final a is the answer.

🎯 Core Concepts

Odd k-th term: The k-th odd number (0-indexed) is 2*k + 1. So odd[0]=1, odd[1]=3, odd[2]=5, ...
Even k-th term: The k-th even number (0-indexed, starting from 2) is 2*(k+1). So even[0]=2, even[1]=4, even[2]=6, ...
Euclidean GCD: gcd(a, b) = gcd(b, a mod b) repeatedly until b=0. Then a is the GCD.
🪄 Math shortcut: sumOdd = n², sumEven = n(n+1). Since n and n+1 are coprime, answer is always n!
Time O(n + log n) Space O(1)
